Acne

Reasons :

  1. Hormonal Changes:

    • Puberty: Increased androgen levels during puberty can cause the sebaceous (oil) glands to enlarge and produce more sebum.
    • Menstrual Cycle: Hormonal fluctuations before and during menstruation can trigger acne flare-ups.
    • Pregnancy: Hormonal changes during pregnancy can lead to acne.
    • Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS): A hormonal disorder that can cause acne due to increased androgen levels.

  2. Excess Oil Production:

    • Viral Infections: Conditions like chickenpox, measles, rubella, and roseola.
  3. Bacterial Infections: Infections such as impetigo, cellulitis, and scarlet fever.

  5. Parasitic Infections: Scabies and lice infestations.

  6. Excess Oil Production:

    • Sebum Overproduction: Excessive production of sebum by the sebaceous glands can clog pores and lead to acne.

  7. Clogged Pores:

    • Dead Skin Cells: Accumulation of dead skin cells can block hair follicles and pores, leading to acne formation.

  8. Diet:

    • High Glycemic Foods: Foods with a high glycemic index, such as sugary snacks and refined carbohydrates, may contribute to acne.
    • Dairy Products: Some studies suggest that consuming dairy products may be linked to acne in certain individuals.

  9. Stress:

    • Stress Hormones: Increased levels of stress can lead to the release of cortisol and other hormones that may exacerbate acne.

  10. Environmental Factors:

    • Pollution: Exposure to environmental pollutants can clog pores and irritate the skin, leading to acne.
    • Humidity: High humidity levels can increase oil production and contribute to acne.

Ayurvedic Remedies :

  1. Turmeric and Milk Paste:

    2. Mix a teaspoon of turmeric powder with enough milk to form a paste. Apply this paste to the acne-affected areas and leave it on for 15-20 minutes before rinsing off with warm water. Turmeric has anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties, while milk soothes and hydrates the skin.

  2. Aloe Vera and Turmeric:

    3. Mix fresh aloe vera gel with a pinch of turmeric powder. Apply this mixture to the acne-prone areas and leave it on for 20 minutes before rinsing off with cool water. Aloe vera soothes and heals the skin, while turmeric reduces inflammation.

  3. Cucumber and Yogurt Mask:

    4. Blend half a cucumber with two tablespoons of yogurt to make a smooth paste. Apply this mask to your face and leave it on for 20 minutes before rinsing off with cool water. Cucumber has cooling properties, and yogurt contains lactic acid that helps to exfoliate and soothe the skin.

  4. Orange Peel Paste:

    5. Dry orange peels in the sun and grind them into a powder. Mix this powder with water to form a paste and apply it to the acne-prone areas. Leave it on for 20 minutes before rinsing off with cool water. Orange peel contains vitamin C and has natural astringent properties that help in reducing acne.

  5. Tea Tree Oil and Aloe Vera Gel:

    6. Mix a few drops of tea tree oil with a tablespoon of aloe vera gel. Apply this mixture to the affected areas and leave it on overnight. Tea tree oil has antibacterial properties, and aloe vera soothes and heals the skin.
