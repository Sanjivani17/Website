Backpain

Reasons :

  1. Muscle or Ligament Strain:

    • Overexertion, heavy lifting, or sudden awkward movements can strain muscles and ligaments, leading to back pain.

  2. Poor Posture:

    • Sitting or standing for long periods with poor posture can cause back pain.

  3. Herniated or Bulging Discs:

    • Discs act as cushions between the vertebrae. When a disc bulges or ruptures, it can press on nerves, causing pain.

  4. Infections or Tumors:

    • Infections in the spine or tumors can lead to back pain.

  5. Injuries:

    • Accidents, falls, or sports injuries can cause back pain.

  6. Sedentary Lifestyle :

    • Lack of physical activity can weaken muscles, contributing to back pain.

Ayurvedic Remedies :

  1. Ginger (Adrak):

    2. Ginger has anti-inflammatory properties. Consuming ginger tea or applying a paste of ginger and water to the affected area can provide relief.

  2. Turmeric (Haldi):

    3. Turmeric contains curcumin, which has anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving properties. Drinking turmeric milk or applying a paste of turmeric and honey can help.

  3. Garlic (Lahsun):

    4. Garlic is known for its anti-inflammatory properties. Consuming garlic in the morning or applying garlic oil to the affected area can be beneficial.

  4. Shallaki (Boswellia):

    5. Boswellia serrata, known as Shallaki in Ayurveda, has anti-inflammatory properties. It can be taken in supplement form to reduce pain and inflammation.

  5. Ashwagandha:

    6. Ashwagandha helps in reducing stress and inflammation. Consuming Ashwagandha powder or capsules can be beneficial for back pain.
