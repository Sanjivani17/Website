HALDI

Botanical Name: Cuminum cyminum


Turmeric (Curcuma longa), known as "haldi" in Hindi, is highly valued in traditional medicine for its medicinal properties. The active compound, curcumin, has strong anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects, aiding in conditions like arthritis and inflammatory bowel disease. Turmeric’s antimicrobial properties help combat infections, while its antioxidants reduce oxidative stress and prevent cell damage. It supports digestive health and can treat skin conditions when applied topically. Additionally, curcumin may inhibit cancer cell growth, support cardiovascular health, and enhance brain health by potentially preventing neurodegenerative diseases. Despite its low bioavailability, turmeric’s health benefits are extensive.

Used For: Arthritis, colds, coughs, sore throats, acne, indigestion, bloating, pain, wounds, diabetes, heart disease.

CUMIN

Botanical Name: Curcuma longa


Cumin (*Cuminum cyminum*) is a flowering plant in the Apiaceae family, native to the eastern Mediterranean region and South Asia. It is cultivated for its aromatic seeds, which are extensively used in culinary traditions worldwide. Cumin seeds are small, elongated, and ridged with a distinctive warm and earthy flavor. They are a staple spice in Indian, Middle Eastern, and Mexican cuisines, adding depth and richness to dishes like curries, stews, and soups. Beyond its culinary uses, cumin is valued for its potential health benefits. It is believed to aid digestion, alleviate gas and bloating, and may have antimicrobial properties. Cumin seeds are also used in traditional medicine for their supposed ability to boost immunity and promote respiratory health. Additionally, cumin oil is utilized in aromatherapy for its soothing and calming effects.

Used For: Indigestion, bloating, diarrhea, respiratory disorders, anemia, insomnia, skin disorders, common cold, asthma.

ALOEVERA

Botanical Name: Aloe barbadensis miller


Aloe vera, botanically known as Aloe barbadensis miller, is a succulent plant prized for its medicinal properties and has been used for centuries in traditional medicine. The gel extracted from its leaves contains vitamins, minerals, amino acids, and antioxidants that provide various health benefits. It's commonly used to soothe skin conditions such as burns, cuts, and eczema due to its anti-inflammatory and moisturizing properties. Additionally, aloe vera aids in digestion, supports immune function, and has been noted for its potential to regulate blood sugar levels. Its versatility makes it a staple in natural health remedies and skincare products.

Used For: Burns, cuts, eczema, psoriasis, acne, sunburn, digestive issues, constipation, diabetes.

NEEM

Botanical Name: Azadirachta indica


Neem (*Azadirachta indica*) is a tree native to the Indian subcontinent, renowned for its multifaceted medicinal properties. It has been a cornerstone of traditional Ayurvedic medicine for centuries. Neem leaves, bark, seeds, and oil are used to treat a variety of ailments due to their anti-inflammatory, antibacterial, antiviral, and antifungal properties. Neem oil is commonly applied to the skin to treat conditions like eczema and psoriasis, while neem leaves are often used in teas to boost immune function and detoxify the body. The tree's natural insect-repellent qualities also make it a popular ingredient in natural pesticides and skin care products. Its extensive use in traditional medicine highlights its importance as a natural remedy for maintaining overall health and well-being.

Used For: Neem is utilized in various home remedies and traditional medicine for its therapeutic properties. It is reputed to be beneficial for treating skin disorders such as acne, eczema, and psoriasis. Additionally, neem is used to alleviate dandruff and other scalp conditions.

ONION

Botonical Name: Allium cepa


Onion (*Allium cepa*) is a staple in culinary traditions worldwide, celebrated for its distinctive flavor and health benefits. Rich in vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants, onions are known to bolster the immune system, reduce inflammation, and promote heart health. They contain quercetin, a powerful antioxidant that helps fight free radicals and may reduce the risk of chronic diseases. Onions also have antibacterial properties, making them useful in preventing infections. In addition to their medicinal properties, onions can help regulate blood sugar levels and improve digestive health. Their versatility in cooking, coupled with their health-promoting qualities, makes onions an indispensable ingredient in both kitchens and natural medicine cabinets.

Used For: Onion is commonly used in home remedies for its medicinal properties. It is believed to be effective in treating conditions such as cough, cold, and sore throat.

GARLIC

Botonical Name: Allium sativum


Garlic (*Allium sativum*) is renowned for its potent medicinal properties and culinary versatility. Rich in sulfur compounds, especially allicin, garlic has been used for centuries to boost the immune system and fight infections. It is known to reduce blood pressure, lower cholesterol levels, and improve heart health, making it a staple in many natural remedies for cardiovascular diseases. Garlic also exhibits anti-inflammatory, antiviral, and antibacterial properties, contributing to its effectiveness in treating colds and other infections. Additionally, it may help detoxify heavy metals in the body and improve bone health. Whether consumed raw, cooked, or in supplement form, garlic's health benefits make it a valuable addition to any diet.

Used For: Garlic is known for its various medicinal uses and is often used in home remedies to treat conditions such as high blood pressure, high cholesterol, and colds.

CLOVE

Clove (*Syzygium aromaticum*) is a highly valued spice known for its distinctive aroma and potent medicinal properties. Additionally, clove oil is often used in aromatherapy to reduce stress and improve mental clarity. This versatile spice, whether used whole or as an oil, plays a significant role in traditional medicine and modern health practices.

Used For: Clove is used in home remedies to help alleviate toothaches and is also known for its antiseptic properties.

AMLA

ASHWAGANDHA

Additionally, ashwagandha is used to boost vitality, improve sleep quality, and support overall wellness, making it a versatile and valued herbal remedy.

Used For: Ashwagandha is used in traditional medicine to help alleviate stress, anxiety, and insomnia.

SHIKAKAI

Botonical Name: Acacia concinna


Shikakai (*Acacia concinna*) is a traditional Ayurvedic herb primarily used in hair care. It is renowned for its natural cleansing and conditioning properties, making it a popular alternative to chemical-based shampoos. Shikakai is rich in saponins, which create a mild lather when mixed with water, effectively cleansing the scalp and hair without stripping away natural oils. It helps to maintain scalp health, promoting stronger hair follicles and reducing dandruff. Shikakai is also known to impart shine and softness to the hair, making it easier to manage. It is often used in combination with other herbs like amla and reetha to enhance its benefits for hair care.

Used For: Shikakai is primarily used in home remedies for hair care, such as promoting hair growth, preventing dandruff, and nourishing the scalp.

Used For: Reetha, also known as soapnut, is commonly used in home remedies for hair care. It is known for its natural cleansing properties, helping to cleanse the scalp and hair effectively.

SPEARMINT

Botonical Name: Mentha spicata


Spearmint (*Mentha spicata*) is a perennial herb known for its refreshing aroma and medicinal properties. It belongs to the mint family and is widely used in culinary and therapeutic applications. Spearmint leaves are rich in essential oils like menthol, which impart a cooling sensation and minty flavor. In traditional medicine, spearmint is valued for its digestive benefits, helping to relieve indigestion, gas, and nausea. It is also known for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties, which may contribute to overall health and wellness. Spearmint tea is a popular herbal remedy for promoting relaxation, soothing sore throats, and supporting respiratory health. Additionally, spearmint essential oil is used in aromatherapy for its invigorating scent and potential stress-relieving effects.

Used For: Spearmint is used in various home remedies and traditional medicine practices. It is known for its potential health benefits in treating digestive disorders, respiratory conditions, and hormonal imbalances.
