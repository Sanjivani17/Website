Kneepain

Reasons :

  1. Osteoarthritis:

    • Degenerative joint disease causing the breakdown of cartilage and leading to pain and stiffness.

  2. Meniscus Tears:

    • Tears in the cartilage that cushions the knee joint, often caused by twisting movements.

  3. Ligament Injuries:

    • Injuries to the ACL, MCL, or other ligaments due to trauma or sports activities.

  4. Patellar Tendonitis (Jumper's Knee):

    • Inflammation of the tendon connecting the kneecap to the shinbone, common in athletes.

  5. Baker's Cyst:

    • A fluid-filled cyst behind the knee, often due to other knee conditions like arthritis.

  6. Chondromalacia Patellae:

    • Softening and breakdown of the cartilage on the underside of the kneecap, causing pain.

Ayurvedic Remedies :

  1. Turmeric (Haldi):

    2. Turmeric contains curcumin, known for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. Consuming turmeric milk or taking turmeric supplements can help reduce knee pain.

  2. Ginger (Adrak):

    3. Ginger has anti-inflammatory properties. Drinking ginger tea or applying ginger paste to the affected knee can provide relief.

  3. Ashwagandha:

    4. Ashwagandha is an adaptogen that helps reduce stress and inflammation. Consuming Ashwagandha powder or capsules can help manage knee pain.

  4. Shallaki (Boswellia Serrata):

    5. Boswellia has anti-inflammatory properties and is used to treat joint pain. Taking Boswellia supplements can be beneficial.

  5. Castor Oil (Eranda):

    6. Castor oil has anti-inflammatory properties. Warm castor oil can be massaged into the knee joint for relief.

  6. Nirgundi (Vitex Negundo):

    7. Nirgundi leaves have analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties. The leaves can be used to prepare a poultice and applied to the affected area.
