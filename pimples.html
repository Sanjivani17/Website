Pimples

Reasons :

  1. Hormonal Changes:

    • Puberty: Increased levels of androgens during puberty cause the sebaceous glands to enlarge and produce more sebum.
    • Menstrual Cycle: Hormonal fluctuations before a menstrual period can lead to breakouts.
    • Pregnancy and Menopause: Changes in hormone levels during pregnancy and menopause can trigger acne.

  2. Excess Sebum Production:

    • Overactive sebaceous glands can produce too much sebum, leading to clogged pores and pimples.

  3. Environmental Factors :

    • Pollution, high humidity, and exposure to certain chemicals or oils can exacerbate acne.

  4. Clogged Pores :

    • Dead skin cells, excess oil, and dirt can clog hair follicles, resulting in blackheads, whiteheads, and pimples.

  5. Genetics :

    • A family history of acne can increase the likelihood of developing pimples.

  6. Cosmetics and Skincare Products :

    • Comedogenic products can clog pores and contribute to acne.

Ayurvedic Remedies :

  1. Lemon Juice and Rose Water:

    2. Mix equal parts of fresh lemon juice and rose water. Apply the mixture to the affected areas using a cotton ball. Leave it on for 15-20 minutes and then rinse with cool water. Lemon juice helps to dry out pimples and reduce scars, while rose water tones and cools the skin.

  2. Turmeric and Honey Paste:

    3. Mix equal parts of turmeric powder and honey to make a paste. Apply it to the affected area and leave it on for 20 minutes before rinsing off with warm water. Turmeric has anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties, while honey is a natural humectant that keeps the skin hydrated.

  3. Neem Leaves Paste:

    4. Crush fresh neem leaves into a paste or use neem powder mixed with water. Apply this paste to the pimples and leave it on for 20 minutes before washing off. Neem is known for its antibacterial and antifungal properties, which help in reducing acne.

  4. Sandalwood and Rose Water:

    5. Mix sandalwood powder with rose water to form a smooth paste. Apply it to your face and let it dry. Wash off with lukewarm water. Sandalwood soothes the skin and reduces inflammation, while rose water acts as a natural toner.

  5. Aloe Vera Gel:

    6. Extract fresh aloe vera gel from the leaf and apply it directly to the pimples. Leave it on overnight and rinse off in the morning. Aloe vera has anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties that help in healing pimples.

  6. Fenugreek Paste:

    7. Soak fenugreek seeds overnight and grind them into a fine paste. Apply the paste to the pimples and leave it on for 20 minutes before rinsing off. Fenugreek has anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties that help reduce acne.

  7. Tulsi (Holy Basil) Leaves:

    8. Crush fresh tulsi leaves to make a paste or use tulsi powder mixed with water. Apply it to the pimples and leave it on for 15-20 minutes before washing off. Tulsi has antibacterial and antifungal properties that help in treating acne.
