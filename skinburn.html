Skin burn

Reasons :

  1. Thermal Burns:

    • Hot Liquids: Burns caused by hot water, coffee, tea, or other liquids.
    • Hot Objects: Contact with hot surfaces, such as stovetops, irons, or heated tools.
    • Flames: Exposure to fire or flames from matches, candles, or fireplaces.

  2. Chemical Burns:

    • Acids: Exposure to strong acids like sulfuric acid, hydrochloric acid, or battery acid.
  3. Bases: Contact with alkaline substances like bleach, ammonia, or lye.
  4. Other Chemicals: Exposure to solvents, detergents, or other harsh chemicals.

  5. Radiation Burns:

    • Sunburn: Overexposure to ultraviolet (UV) radiation from the sun.
    • Radiation Therapy: Burns caused by radiation treatment for cancer
    • Other Sources: Exposure to other forms of radiation, such as X-rays.

  6. Microwave Burns:

    • Superheated Water: Burns caused by superheated water or food items that explode upon removal from the microwave.

  7. Flash Burns:

    • Welding: Burns from the intense UV light produced by welding.
    • Explosions: Burns caused by the flash of an explosion or other intense light source.

  8. Burns from Skin Care Products:

    • Harsh Ingredients: Burns from products containing strong acids, retinoids, or other irritants.
    • Allergic Reactions: Burns from allergic reactions to ingredients in skin care products.

Ayurvedic Remedies :

  1. Turmeric and Aloe Vera Gel:

    2. Mix equal parts of turmeric powder and fresh aloe vera gel to make a paste. Apply it to the affected area and leave it on for 20 minutes before rinsing off with cool water. Turmeric has anti-inflammatory properties, and aloe vera soothes and heals burnt skin.

  2. Neem Oil and Coconut Oil:

    3. Mix a few drops of neem oil with coconut oil. Apply this mixture to the burned area and leave it on for at least 30 minutes before gently washing off with cool water. Neem oil has antibacterial properties, and coconut oil moisturizes and promotes healing.

  3. Sandalwood and Rose Water:

    4. Mix sandalwood powder with rose water to form a smooth paste. Apply it to the burned area and let it dry. Wash off with cool water. Sandalwood soothes the skin and reduces inflammation, while rose water has cooling and hydrating properties.

  4. Honey and Lavender Oil:

    5. Mix equal parts of honey and a few drops of lavender essential oil. Apply this mixture to the burn and cover with a sterile bandage. Leave it on for several hours or overnight. Honey is a natural humectant and has antibacterial properties, while lavender oil promotes healing and reduces pain.

  5. Cucumber and Potato Paste:

    6. Blend cucumber and potato to make a smooth paste. Apply it to the burned area and leave it on for 20-30 minutes before rinsing off with cool water. Cucumber has cooling properties, and potato helps reduce inflammation and soothes the skin.

  6. Ghee and Turmeric:

    7. Mix a teaspoon of ghee (clarified butter) with a pinch of turmeric powder. Apply this mixture to the burn and leave it on for 20 minutes before gently washing off with cool water. Ghee moisturizes and promotes healing, while turmeric reduces inflammation.
