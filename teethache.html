Teethache

Reasons :

  1. Tooth Decay (Cavities):

    • Decay caused by bacteria that produce acids that damage the tooth enamel.

  2. Gum Disease (Periodontal Disease):

    • Infections of the structures around the teeth, including the gums, periodontal ligament, and alveolar bone.

  3. Tooth Abscess:

    • A pocket of pus caused by bacterial infection, leading to severe pain and swelling.

  4. Worn Tooth Enamel:

    • Enamel erosion caused by acid erosion, bruxism (teeth grinding), or overzealous brushing.

  5. Tooth Sensitivity:

    • Sensitivity to hot, cold, sweet, or acidic foods and drinks due to exposed dentin.

  6. Impacted Wisdom Teeth:

    • Wisdom teeth that do not fully emerge or align properly can cause pain.

Ayurvedic Remedies :

  1. Clove Oil (Lavang):

    2. Clove oil has analgesic and antiseptic properties. Applying clove oil to the affected tooth can provide relief from pain.

  2. Garlic (Lahsun):

    3. Garlic has antibacterial properties. Placing a crushed garlic clove on the affected tooth can help reduce pain and infection.

  3. Turmeric (Haldi):

    4. Turmeric has anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties. Applying a paste of turmeric and water to the affected area can help relieve pain.

  4. Peppermint Tea Bags:

    5. Peppermint has a numbing effect. Placing a cooled peppermint tea bag on the affected tooth can help relieve pain.

  5. Saltwater Rinse:

    6. Rinsing the mouth with warm salt water can help reduce inflammation and clean the affected area.

  6. Asafoetida (Hing):

    7. Hing has anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. Applying a paste of hing and lemon juice to the affected tooth can help reduce pain.
